Research Methods

Methodology

The Observatory uses documented search, screening, and classification procedures to map an interdisciplinary body of scholarship.

Research objective

The project seeks to identify, organize, and analyze scholarship relevant to intellectual property questions created or intensified by autonomous and AI-enabled aerospace systems.

Scope

Sources may address aerospace systems directly or supply a legal, technical, or policy framework with a clear application to autonomous aerospace innovation.

Preliminary inclusion criteria

  • The source substantially addresses intellectual property, innovation law, ownership, licensing, technology transfer, or another closely related legal issue.
  • The source substantially addresses autonomous systems, artificial intelligence, machine learning, adaptive control, automated engineering design, or a related technology.
  • The source concerns aerospace directly or has a clearly documented application to aerospace autonomy.
  • Sufficient bibliographic information is available to identify and verify the source.

Preliminary exclusion criteria

  • Brief news articles with no original legal, technical, or policy analysis.
  • Marketing material that provides no meaningful evidence or analytical contribution.
  • General discussions of AI or aerospace with no credible connection to intellectual property or innovation governance.
  • Duplicate versions of the same work, unless separate versions contain substantively different information.

Classification

Each source is classified by source type, aerospace domain, autonomy type, intellectual property issue, jurisdiction, keywords, and relevance to the Observatory.

Limitations

Because the project spans several disciplines, terminology and database coverage vary substantially. The database should be understood as a developing map of the field, not as a claim that every relevant source has been identified.

Updates and corrections

Entries may be revised as classifications improve, errors are identified, or new versions of sources become available.
