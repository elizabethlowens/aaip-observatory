Autonomous Aerospace and Intellectual Property

Mapping an emerging interdisciplinary field

The AAIP Observatory collects, classifies, and analyzes scholarship concerning intellectual property issues raised by autonomous and AI-enabled aerospace systems.

Explore the Database Read the Methodology

Purpose

Relevant research is distributed across aerospace engineering, artificial intelligence, patent law, innovation policy, administrative law, and technology governance. The Observatory brings these conversations together in a structured, searchable resource.

Areas of inquiry

Inventorship

Human contribution, AI-assisted invention, autonomous design, and the legal definition of an inventor.

Patent disclosure

Enablement, written description, reproducibility, and disclosure of adaptive or learning systems.

Ownership and licensing

Rights in machine-generated designs, software, data, models, and outputs.

Innovation governance

The relationship among intellectual property, regulation, certification, standards, safety, and technology transfer.

Project development

The database is continuously updated. Periodic archived releases and analytical reports may preserve and analyze specific versions of the dataset.
